Motivation: AI for Indian Languages

India has 26 official languages, many of which are low-resource languages.

What is a Low-Resource Language?

  • Not enough training data available
  • AI models struggle to understand them properly
Example Problem: If you ask a question in a rare Indian language using tools like ChatGPT or Gemini, the model may fail to understand it.

The Solution

Researchers are working on:

  • Building datasets for Indian languages
  • Creating language resources for all 26 languages
  • Training AI models specifically for Indian linguistic diversity
Goal: Make Generative AI usable for Indian languages, not just English.

What is Artificial Intelligence?

Artificial Intelligence means creating machines that can perform tasks requiring human intelligence.

How Human Intelligence Works

Humans are intelligent because our brain contains neurons:

  • Connected through synapses
  • Transfer information
  • Control reasoning, thinking, and decisions
AI Approach: AI tries to mimic this neuron structure using Artificial Neural Networks.

Large Language Models (LLMs)

Large Language Models are AI systems that understand and generate human language.

Popular Examples

  • ChatGPT
  • Gemini
  • Claude
  • DeepSeek

Why "Large"?

These models are trained on huge datasets from:

  • Wikipedia
  • News articles
  • Books
  • Websites
  • Social media
  • Online documents
Interesting Fact: These models may see more text during training than a human reads in their entire life.

Core Architecture of LLMs

Most modern LLMs use the Transformer architecture, which has two main parts:

1. Encoder

  • Processes the input (sentence, image, etc.)
  • Converts input into a vector representation

2. Decoder

  • Uses encoded information to generate output
  • Outputs: text, image description, summary, translation
Visual Language Model Example:
Image → Encoder → Vectors → Decoder → "A dog playing in a park."

Why LLMs are Different from Traditional ML

Traditional ML: Requires a separate model for every task

  • Tumor classification
  • Fraud detection
  • Disease detection
  • Each needs its own dataset and training

LLMs Have General Intelligence

You can give them a new task with only a few examples. This ability is called Few-shot learning.

This is a fundamental difference that makes LLMs more versatile and efficient.

Types of Language Models

Language models are categorized by number of parameters (internal weights the model learns).

Type Parameters Characteristics Examples
Small < 100B Easier to run locally, can be fine-tuned by universities LLaMA small models
Large 100B+ Require massive computing power, usually owned by companies GPT-4, Gemini, Claude

History of Artificial Intelligence

1950 — Turing Test
Proposed by Alan Turing to check whether a machine behaves like a human. If a person cannot distinguish between a human and a computer in conversation, the machine passes.
1956 — Dartmouth Conference
Birth of AI. John McCarthy introduced the term "Artificial Intelligence." Researchers began discussing machine intelligence, automation, and neural networks.
1980 — Expert Systems
AI systems using multiple models together — similar to modern multi-agent/agentic AI — where different components collaborate to solve problems.
1997 — IBM Deep Blue
AI chess program defeated world champion Garry Kasparov — a major milestone proving AI could outperform humans in specific tasks.
2012 — Deep Learning Revolution
Triggered by cheaper GPUs and larger datasets. AlexNet demonstrated powerful image recognition and computer vision capabilities.
2017 — Transformer Architecture
Google published "Attention Is All You Need," introducing the Transformer — the foundation of modern LLMs.

Attention Mechanism

When processing long sentences, models struggle to understand relationships.

Example: "Shekhar who worked for many years in Delhi started his own business in Patna." — To understand that "he" refers to Shekhar, the model must track long-distance relationships.

How Attention Helps

Attention helps models focus on important words and ignore irrelevant ones, enabling better understanding of context and relationships.

Rise of Generative AI

2020: GPT-3 released — the first large generative model widely used.
2022: ChatGPT launched with a user-friendly interface (no Python code needed — anyone can type a prompt).
2025 (Current Era): Rise of Vision Language Models, Video Models, Audio Models, and Agentic AI. AI can now generate images, music, video, text, and code.

Agentic AI

Agentic AI systems contain multiple AI agents working together, each with a specific job.

Example: Cricket Strategy System

Agent Task
Agent 1 Collect match data
Agent 2 Analyze player statistics
Agent 3 Calculate probabilities
Agent 4 Suggest strategy

All agents communicate and produce a final decision.

Reasoning Models

Traditional LLMs are good at text generation. Reasoning models aim to solve:

  • Mathematical problems
  • Logical puzzles
  • Financial analysis

Examples

  • DeepSeek
  • OpenAI O1

These models use Reinforcement Learning for improved reasoning.

Reinforcement Learning

The model receives feedback during training:

Feedback Mechanism:
• If the model solves a math problem incorrectly → penalty
• If correct → reward

The model learns the best reasoning path over time.

Reinforcement Learning with Human Feedback (RLHF)

Humans evaluate model outputs — the model produces two answers, humans choose the better one, and the model learns preferences.

Outcome: This process improves alignment and model quality.

Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG)

Sometimes models hallucinate (generate wrong answers). RAG solves this by:

  • Searching the internet/database
  • Retrieving relevant information
  • Generating the answer using that information
Benefit: This approach reduces hallucination significantly.

In-Context Learning

LLMs can learn tasks during inference without retraining.

How it Works: You provide a few examples in the prompt, and the LLM learns the pattern from those examples to solve a new one.

Ethical Issues in AI

AI misuse is increasing. Key concerns include:

  • Fake content and deepfakes
  • Bias in AI models
  • Privacy issues

Regulatory Response

Because of these concerns:

  • The EU introduced the AI Act (2024)
  • Institutes now require ethical clearance before AI research

Current Research Directions in AI

1. Model Distillation

Reducing model size while maintaining performance.

2. Mechanistic Interpretability

Understanding what happens inside each layer of the model. Goal: remove unnecessary layers.

3. Edge Deployment

Making smaller, efficient models that can run on small devices (not just large cloud infrastructure).

Future of AI

Researchers aim for Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) — AI capable of performing any intellectual task a human can do.

Future AI Systems Will Have

  • Reasoning and decision making
  • Self-learning capabilities
  • Autonomous agents
This represents the ultimate goal of AI research and development.
